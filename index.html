The Wicher

The Witcher is a dark fantasy series based on Andrzej Sapkowski’s novels. The main character is Geralt of Rivia, a Witcher — a mutant monster hunter created through genetic experiments. His job is to kill dangerous monsters for money.

The story takes place in a medieval world where humans, elves, dwarves, and monsters coexist. But often, the real monsters are people themselves. The series explores politics, war, racism, and moral gray areas. Nothing is purely good or evil.

Geralt’s life is tied to two women: Yennefer of Vengerberg, a powerful sorceress with whom he has a complicated love story, and Ciri, the princess of Cintra who becomes his adopted daughter through the Law of Surprise. Ciri carries ancient Elder Blood that could change the fate of the world.

The Netflix series stars Henry Cavill as Geralt. Both the games and books are famous for mature storytelling, tough choices, and deep worldbuilding. Every decision has consequences. If you like Game of Thrones-style stories with magic, sword fights, and moral dilemmas, The Witcher is worth watching.

LUCY (2014) is a sci-fi action thriller directed by Luc Besson and starring Scarlett Johansson in the lead role. The story follows Lucy, an ordinary young woman who gets caught up in a drug smuggling operation in Taipei. She is forced to carry a powerful synthetic drug called CPH4 inside her body. When the bag leaks, the drug enters her system and unlocks her brain capacity far beyond the normal 10% limit. As the drug takes effect, Lucy’s mind evolves at an incredible rate. She gains superhuman abilities like telekinesis, instant learning, time perception, and complete control over her body and mind. With each passing moment, her intelligence and powers grow, but her emotions and humanity start to fade. She becomes less human and more like a living supercomputer, racing against time to understand the true meaning of existence. The film blends fast-paced action with deep philosophical questions about consciousness, evolution, and the purpose of life. Morgan Freeman plays Professor Norman, a neuroscientist who helps explain the science behind Lucy’s transformation. LUCY is visually stunning, thought-provoking, and full of twists. It explores the idea of what would happen if humans could use 100% of their brain. The ending leaves viewers debating whether it’s pure science fiction or a glimpse into the future of human potential.
